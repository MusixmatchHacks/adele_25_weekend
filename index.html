Search and listening trends: Adele's 25

By Varun Jewalikar and Federica Fragapane at Musixmatch

Adele's '25' was released last Friday, 20th November. Let us look at some interesting search and listening/reading (lyrics) trends for the album over the weekend.

Search trends

Google trends is an interesting tool for comparing google search volumes of various terms. Even though the exact volume of searches is not specified but we can still make some interesting relative comparisons.

Over the weekend

Comparing these trends we see that all these searches start peaking out on release date which could indicate the listeners looking for lyrics, music, videos and information related to the new album. The lyrics search volume is 20% higher than 'adele album' and almost 3 times higher than 'adele youtube'. People are really interested in reading these lyrics.

The last 2 months

In general, the google search trend for lyrics is always higher than the other two. On October 23rd the first single from the album 'Hello' was released and we see a massive jump in all the searches. The lyrics search trend on 24th October is almost 5 times higher than 'adele album' and 'adele youtube'. A similar trend is seen on November 17th when the second single When we were young was released. All in all, lyrics are a very important part of the listening experience.

Torrents to the rescue of streaming subscribers ?

This interesting trend was spotted by Andy Weissman. The torrent searches start peaking out on release date. One reason could be that Adele chose to not release the album on streaming services which causes the listeners to search for alternatives elsewhere.

Listening and reading trends

Musixmatch's apps are amongst the most used music apps worldwide. Also, our website is one of the top lyrics sources on the internet. From both of these we gather a lot of listening/lyrics reading data (anonymised) every minute. Here we zoom in on this data over the last weekend for Adele's new album.

Overall

From 19th November(Thursday) to 22nd November(Sunday), our users have spent 4364 hours (~182 days) listening to the songs and reading this album's lyrics from our apps and website. In the next section we break this down by track and platform.

Analysis by track and platform

The engagement in our apps is much higher than our website. The engagement everyday over the weekend is remains similar on both the platforms() except for the peak on our website on Friday (release day).

'Hello' is by far the more listened song from this album right now. Along with it the other single 'When we were young' are driving more engagement than the other 7 songs.

Conclusion

Adele's fans were eagerly waiting for this album since 2011, the huge search and listening patterns indicate that this could indeed be a very popular album. The above search trends also show the immense importance of lyrics for music fans.
